The purpose of a report text is to present information about something generally. It generally describes the way things are with reference to arrange of natural, manmade and social phenomenon in our environment, such as: mammals, the planets, rocks, plants, countries of region, cultures, transportations, and so on

Generic structures
• Title
A title of a report text indicates the topic of the text and it is a very general thing.

A general classification is a part that state classification of general aspect of things, such as: animals, public places, plants, etc. It will be discussed in general.

This part gives describing of the things which will be discussed in detail, in terms of: parts (and their function), qualities, habits and behavior.

13. Buatlah contoh text cerita rakyat, legenda dan mitos dalam bahasa inggris beserta social function, generic structure and language feature​


Title: The Legend of Malin Kundang

Social Function: To entertain and educate readers about a famous Indonesian legend, imparting moral lessons about filial piety and consequences of one's actions.

Generic Structure:

Orientation: Introduce the setting, characters, and initial situation.

Complication: Present the conflict or dilemma faced by the main character.

Resolution: Describe the climax and its consequences.

Reorientation: Conclude the story and deliver the moral lesson.


Language Features:

Use of descriptive language to create imagery and evoke emotions.

Dialogue between characters to convey their thoughts and emotions.

Cultural and local references to establish the setting.

Repetition of moral lessons for emphasis.

Use of traditional storytelling devices like repetition and imagery.

The Legend of Malin Kundang

Once upon a time, in a small coastal village in West Sumatra, there lived a young man named Malin Kundang. He was known for his handsome looks and his dream to escape his humble origins and seek wealth and glory in far-off lands. His mother, a hardworking and loving woman, always warned him to stay humble and never forget his roots.

One day, Malin Kundang set sail on a grand voyage, promising his mother that he would return home as a successful man. Years passed, and he amassed wealth beyond imagination. He married a beautiful princess and became a rich and powerful king.

As he sailed back to his village with his lavish entourage, he saw his mother waiting by the shore. Ashamed of his humble origins, he ordered his crew to sail away, ignoring his mother's desperate cries for recognition.

Suddenly, a massive storm swept over the sea. Lightning struck his ship, and it turned to stone. Malin Kundang and his crew were transformed into rocks for his disobedience and disrespect toward his mother.

Moral of the Story:

This legend teaches us the importance of respecting our parents and not letting pride and arrogance overshadow our roots. It also conveys the idea that actions have consequences, and that one should never forget where they come from.

16. What is'ICW"? What is its function?


Penjelasan:

ICW stands for Indonesian Corruption Watch, which is a non-profit organization that focuses on combating corruption in Indonesia. It was founded in 1998, shortly after the fall of the New Order regime, with the aim of promoting transparency and accountability in government and business.

The main function of ICW is to monitor and investigate corruption cases in Indonesia, and to advocate for policies and legal reforms that can help prevent corruption and punish corrupt officials. ICW also provides legal assistance and support to victims of corruption, and works with civil society organizations, media, and other stakeholders to raise public awareness about the negative impact of corruption on society.

In addition, ICW conducts research and publishes reports on corruption issues, and engages in advocacy campaigns to promote transparency, accountability, and good governance. Through its work, ICW has become one of the leading anti-corruption organizations in Indonesia, and has contributed significantly to the fight against corruption in the country.

21. Mention Imperative sentence based on its function


Jawaban:

Declarative sentences state an idea. They end with a period. For example:

The first toilet ever seen on television was on Leave It to Beaver.

The problem with the gene pool is that there's no lifeguard.

Exclamatory sentences show strong emotions. They end with an exclamation mark. For example:

What a mess this room is!

The cake is ruined!

Interrogative sentences ask a question. As you would expect, they end with a question mark. For instance:

Strictly Speaking

Which type of sentence often omits the subject? Imperative sentences, because the subject is often understood, as shown in these examples: “Clean up this mess” or “Help!”

How you gonna keep 'em down on the farm when they've seen Paris?

Why is it possible to tickle someone else but not to tickle yourself?

Imperative sentences give orders or directions, and so end with a period or an exclamation mark. For instance:

Sit down and listen!

Fasten your seatbelts when the sign is illuminated.
