4. 1.what is the difference between likes and likes? Give an example! 2.What's the difference between dislike and dislikes?Give an example! Arti: 1.Apa bedanya like dengan likes? sebutkan contohnya! 2.Apa bedanya dislike dengan dislikes? sebutkan contohnya! Di jawab ya kak


Penjelasan:

1.Like, sebagai kata kerja, berarti suka/menyukai. Penggunaan like atau likes tergantung pada subject-nya. Bila subject tunggal, menggunakan likes. Bila subject jamak, menggunakan like.

2.

Expression of like means expression or phrase that states like / happy about something. Expressing like means expressing love.

Expression of dislike means expression or phrase that states do not like / hate towards something. Expressing dislike means expressing distaste.

EXPRESSION OF LIKE

Pola :

• Subject + like/ like(s) + object (noun/verb-ing)

• Subject + to be + crazy about + object (noun/verb-ing)

• Subject + love/ love(s) + object (noun/verb-ing)

Contoh kalimat:

• I like working with you

• He is crazy everything about you

• She loves eating pizza

EXPRESSION OF DISLIKE

Pola:

• Subject + dislike/ dislike(s) + object (noun/verb-ing)

• Subject + don’t like/ doesn’t like + object (noun/verb-ing)

• Subject + hate/ hate(s) + object (noun/verb-ing)

• Subject + to be + fed up with + object (noun/verb-ing)

• Subject + can’t stand with + object (noun/verb-ing)

Contoh kalimat:

• He really dislikes Korean

• We don’t like sleeping in the afternoon

• I can’t stand with pepper

• He hates lesson English

• The boys are fed up with these cute

30. buatlah 5 kalimat positif ( like or likes ) beserta artinya dan kalimat negatif (dislike or dislikes ) berserta artinya​


Jawaban:

positif

She likes ice cream. (Dia suka es krim.) - Artinya, dia menikmati es krim.

I like spending time with my friends. (Saya suka menghabiskan waktu dengan teman-teman saya.) - Artinya, saya menikmati menghabiskan waktu bersama teman-teman saya.

They like to travel to new places. (Mereka suka bepergian ke tempat-tempat baru.) - Artinya, mereka menikmati perjalanan ke tempat-tempat baru.

Negatif

1. She dislikes ice cream. (Dia tidak suka es krim.) - Artinya, dia tidak menyukai es krim.

2. I don't like spending time with my friends. (Saya tidak suka menghabiskan waktu dengan teman-teman saya.) - Artinya, saya tidak menikmati menghabiskan waktu bersama teman-teman saya.

3. They don't like to travel to new places. (Mereka tidak suka bepergian ke tempat-tempat baru.) - Artinya, mereka tidak menikmati perjalanan ke tempat-tempat baru.

4. He doesn't really like the new book he's reading. (Dia tidak terlalu menyukai buku baru yang sedang dia baca.) - Artinya, dia tidak terlalu menikmati buku baru yang dia baca.

5. We dislike the taste of homemade pizza. (Kami tidak suka rasa pizza buatan sendiri.) - Artinya, kami tidak menyukai rasa pizza buatan sendiri.

Adding "s" to a verb for one person is a part of the conjugation rules in English, specifically for the third person singular. It's a way to match the verb form with the subject in the present tense. This rule helps indicate the subject's number (singular) and person (third person) in the sentence. It's a fundamental aspect of English grammar.

The use of "doesn't" instead of "don't" depends on whether the subject is singular or plural.

1. "Doesn't" is used with a singular subject, including a name:

- Alice doesn't like chocolate.

2. "Don't" is used with plural subjects:

- They don't like chocolate.

The choice between "doesn't" and "don't" is determined by the number (singular or plural) of the subject. In the case of a name like "Alice," it's a singular subject, so you use "doesn't." If you're talking about multiple people or things, you would use "don't" with a plural subject.

Note :

Second person singular = You

Third person singular = He/She/It

First person plural = We

Second person plural = You

Third person plural = They
