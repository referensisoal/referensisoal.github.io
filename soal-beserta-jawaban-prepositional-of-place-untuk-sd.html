34. prepositional phrase


A prepositional phrase is a group of words that lacks either a verb or a subject, and that functions as a unified part of speech. It normally consists of a preposition and a nounor a preposition and a pronoun.
Remember the following rules for prepositional phrases and you will find that using them becomes much easier.

Prepositional phrases always consist of two basic parts at minimum: the object and the preposition.

In formal English, prepositions are almost always followed by objects.

Adjectives can be placed between the prepositions and objects in prepositional phrases.

Prepositional phrases can act as adverbs or adjectives. When they are used as adjectives, they modify nouns and pronouns in the same way single-word adjectives do.

When prepositional phrases are used as adverbs, they at the same way single-word adverbs and adverb clauses do, modifying adjectives, verbs, and other adverbs.

Just like bread and cheese are the minimum ingredients for making a cheese sandwich, a preposition and the object of a preposition are the minimum requirements for forming a prepositional phrase. The object of a preposition is the name for the noun or pronoun following the preposition.
Prepositional phrases don’t have to be plain. Just as adding more ingredients to a sandwich dresses it up, adding more words such as adverbs or adjectives is a good method for spicing up prepositional phrases. As you read the following examples of prepositional phrases, you’ll notice that the first sentence in each set contains a basic prepositional phrase, while the second one contains a more interesting one.

Examples of Prepositional Phrases

The following sentences contain examples of prepositional phrases; the prepositional phrase in each sentence is italicized for easy identification.
The cupcake with sprinkles is yours.
The cupcake with colorful sprinkles is yours.
 
We climbed up the hill.
We climbed up the very steep hill.
 
The rabbits hopped through the garden.
The rabbits hopped through the perfectly manicured garden.

38. Contoh noun of place + that … dan noun of place + where …


Jawaban:

Contoh noun of place + that:

1. City that: Kota yang

Contoh: London is the city that I want to visit. (London adalah kota yang ingin saya kunjungi.)

2. Park that: Taman yang

Contoh: Central Park is the park that I enjoy going to. (Central Park adalah taman yang saya nikmati untuk dikunjungi.)

3. Restaurant that: Restoran yang

Contoh: The restaurant that we went to last night had amazing food. (Restoran yang kami kunjungi semalam memiliki makanan yang luar biasa.)

Contoh noun of place + where:

1. City where: Kota tempat

Contoh: Paris is the city where I was born. (Paris adalah kota tempat saya lahir.)

2. School where: Sekolah tempat

Contoh: The school where I studied is known for its academic excellence. (Sekolah tempat saya belajar terkenal akan keunggulan akademiknya.)

3. Park where: Taman tempat

Contoh: Hyde Park is the park where I like to go for a walk. (Hyde Park adalah taman tempat saya suka pergi berjalan-jalan.)

Jawaban:

berikut adalah beberapa contoh kalimat menggunakan "noun of place + that" dan "noun of place + where":

Noun of Place + That:

1. "The park that we used to play in is being renovated."

2. "The restaurant that we had dinner last night serves amazing pasta."

3. "The city that I was born in has a rich history."

4. "The library that we studied at is open 24 hours."

5. "The beach that we visited last summer is absolutely beautiful."

Noun of Place + Where:

1. "The park where we used to play is being renovated."

2. "The restaurant where we had dinner last night serves amazing pasta."

3. "The city where I was born has a rich history."

4. "The library where we studied at is open 24 hours."

5. "The beach where we visited last summer is absolutely beautiful."

Perhatikan bahwa "that" dan "where" bisa digunakan secara bergantian dalam banyak kasus ini, tetapi "where" lebih umum digunakan untuk merujuk pada tempat atau lokasi. Jangan lupa hatinya ❤️
