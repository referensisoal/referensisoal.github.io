7. contoh teks percakapan inggris asking purpose and asking for purposetolong jawab cepat ​


Contoh 1 - Asking Purpose (Mengajukan Tujuan):

A: Hey, what are you doing this weekend? (Hei, apa yang akan kamu lakukan akhir pekan ini?)

B: I'm going to a charity event on Saturday. (Aku akan pergi ke acara amal pada hari Sabtu.)

A: Oh, that's great! What's the purpose of the event? (Oh, bagus! Apa tujuan dari acara tersebut?)

Contoh 2 - Asking for Purpose (Meminta Tujuan):

A: I'm organizing a meeting next week. (Aku sedang mengatur pertemuan minggu depan.)

B: That sounds interesting. Can you tell me the purpose of the meeting? (Terdengar menarik. Bisakah kamu memberitahu saya tujuan dari pertemuan tersebut?)

A: Sure, the purpose is to discuss our new project strategy. (Tentu, tujuannya adalah untuk membahas strategi proyek baru kita.)

15. contoh teks dialog bahasa inggris asking for attention

Pelajaran : Bahasa Inggris
Kategori : Asking for attention
Kata Kunci : dialog asking for attention

Pembahasan:
In the kitchen
Your mother : Son, are you in your room? (asking for attention)
You : Yes, Mom.
Your mother : Come here, please! (asking for attention)
You : Wait a moment, Mom.
Your mother : Can you buy me eggs and flour? (asking for help)

36. Contoh dialog asking for certainty dalam bahasa Inggris


Jawaban:  It is very common to ask for certainty in daily conversation. There are many kinds of situation where we need to ask for other people certainty. One example is when we're inviting our friends to do something together, it's important to ask whether our friends are sure to go with us or not.

Let's learn some of useful expressions we can use to ask for certainty.

- Are you sure about that?

- Is that so?

- Do you think so?

- Are you certain?

In other situation, we might have to answer other people's answer about our certainty of something. You can use some of these phrases to answer certainty/uncertainty.

Expressing certainty:

- Of course.

- I'm sure about it.

- I have no doubt about that.

Expressing uncertainty:

- I'm not quite sure about that.

- I doubt it.

- It's very unlikely.

- I don't think it's true.

- I don't think so.

So, after learning those basic phrases, let's try incorporating the phrases into a dialog! It's a conversation between Mira and Dina during school break.

Mira: It's Anita's birthday tomorrow. I think we should buy a present for her.

Dina: I think so too! Do you know what we should get for her?

Mira: Hmm... I think we should buy a cake for her.

Dina: That's a great idea. She really likes eating sweets, right? But what kind of cake should we get?

Mira: Yeah... She really likes chocolate cakes.

Dina: Are you sure?

Mira: I'm pretty sure. She told me about that before.

Dina: Okay! How about we go to the cake shop after school today?

Mira: No problem! I'll see you after school today.

Dina: Got it. See you!
